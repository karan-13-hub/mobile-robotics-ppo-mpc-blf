1 · Problem

Aerial manipulation is where learned control stops being safe

  • Base: Skydio X2 quadrotor. Payload: 3-DoF arm → time-varying mass distribution and COM offset.
  • Task: follow a randomly-sampled 7th-order min-snap trajectory to a 15 cm goal tolerance, then hover.
  • PPO alone tracks well on average but transient peaks blow past the safety tube and a folded arm can crash it outright.
  • We want the learned reactivity and a provable envelope — without retraining the policy.

1 · Problem

Three concrete failure modes we targeted

① Lateral tube violations

During aggressive min-snap segments PPO peaks at 22 cm lateral error — 83% bigger than our 12 cm corridor.

② Wind gusts

A 2.5 N lateral pulse (wind-high) drives the baseline into a 52 cm post-pulse swing; it never settles within 10 s.

③ Arm folding (COM shift)

Holding the arm at [0, −1.5, 1.5] rad shifts the COM off-axis. PPO crashes after ~12 s; it was never trained on this.

Goal: keep the base-position error strictly inside a δ-tube at every control step, and stay close to PPO whenever PPO is already safe.

2 · Approach

Architecture: PPO proposes, MPC-BLF filters

 reference            PPO policy              MPC-BLF filter          MuJoCo
  min-snap     ─▶  (learned, reactive)  ─▶  (model-based, safe)  ─▶   simulation
  (p*, v*)           u_ppo ∈ R⁷              u_safe ∈ R⁷              12-state

PPO — reactive layer

  • Gymnasium env, 7-D action (4 rotors + 3 arm joints).
  • Obs: qpos + qvel + relative pose/vel to next ref point.
  • Trained 5 M + 2 M steps with SB3 PPO, 16 parallel envs.
  • Deterministic eval at ~0.9 ms per step.

MPC-BLF — safety layer

  • Receding horizon NLP over the full 12-state nonlinear quad (pos, vel, Euler, body-rate).
  • Solves for U0..N-1 that minimise ‖U0 − uppo‖² — closest safe thing to what PPO asked for.
  • Only touches the 4 rotors; arm joints pass through.
2 · Approach

The Barrier Lyapunov safety constraint

V(e) = ‖e‖² / (δ² − ‖e‖²),    e = p − p*
V(e_{k+1}) ≤ β_k · V(e_k) + σ,   σ ≥ 0,  k = 0…N−1

β_start = 0.95   (loose, near-term: errors are ≈ unavoidable)
β_end   = 0.15   (strict, horizon end: force contraction)
ρ·σ²    in cost  (ρ = 1e3 – 3e3)
  • Finite V ⇔ strictly inside the δ-tube → V→∞ near the boundary makes it impossible to choose a plan that exits.
  • Softened descent: scalar slack σ absorbs the motor-envelope infeasibility, so the NLP almost never aborts.
  • Velocity-aware barrier: z = ‖ep‖² + α‖ev‖², penalises approaching the tube with speed.

What the filter guarantees

  • When σ ≈ 0, the planned trajectory is forward-invariant in the δ-tube over the horizon.
  • Hard fence ‖e‖ ≤ outer_tube keeps V well-defined everywhere the NLP can wander.
  • Fallback to raw uppo if the hard constraints become infeasible — measured rate ≤ 0.62%.

Solver choice

Fatrop (structure-exploiting IP for OCPs) beat IPOPT by ~3× in median solve time and SQP in robustness.

4 · What didn't / still doesn't work

Honest limitations

4a. Compute cost is real

Fatrop + stride=3 gives a 33% duty cycle and ~40 ms median solve. Stride=1 is needed for the tightest tracking, but doubles compute.

4b. Things I tried that failed

  • Zero-order hold between stride solves destabilised the inner attitude loop — replaced with "apply raw PPO between solves".
  • Small-angle linearised dynamics in the MPC: plans disagreed with MuJoCo during aggressive banked turns. Fixed with the full R(φ, θ, ψ) thrust rotation + Coriolis.
  • Hard BLF descent (no slack): NLP went infeasible at motor saturation. Scalar slack σ with heavy ρ·σ² penalty cured it without loosening the tube.
  • Position-only barrier: drone coasted through the setpoint. Velocity-aware barrier + stage-wise velocity cost broke the indifference.
  • PPO-only disturbance training: OOD arm-fold regime still crashes; the filter is what rescues it, not better data.
5 · Challenges & lessons

Things I thought would be trivial that weren't

Sign stability of ZYX Euler from MuJoCo quaternions

Double-cover flips in qw caused the MPC to see ±180° "jumps" and plan huge corrective torques. Fix: canonicalise qw ≥ 0 before extracting Euler angles.

MuJoCo actuator allocation ≠ "textbook" X-config

The M matrix has to come from actuator_gear × site_pos. Hard-coding a symmetric X-config silently offset τz, giving yaw drift in long episodes.

Warm-starting CasADi is make-or-break

Cold start: ~140 ms and occasional Fatrop stalls. Warm start from previous solution: ~40 ms median and almost no fallbacks.

Tuning a soft barrier is a 3-D fight

(β-schedule, ρ, α) all trade off solve time vs. aggressiveness vs. damping. Separate grid sweeps per regime (trajectory vs. disturbance) were needed; one-size-fits-all was worse at both.

6 · Anything else

Takeaways for future learned-controller users

  • Filter, don't re-train. A thin model-based safety layer fixed a catastrophic OOD failure (arm-fold) without touching PPO weights. Cheaper than curriculum + wind randomisation during training.
  • Barrier Lyapunov > plain CBF here. A BLF is its own invariance certificate; you get a forward-invariant set with one inequality per stage, no extra class-𝒦 tuning.
  • Velocity-aware barriers matter for moving setpoints. A position-only barrier lets the drone coast through the goal at speed; z = ‖ep‖² + α‖ev‖² removes that degeneracy.
  • Fatrop + warm-start got a real-time-ish receding-horizon NLP onto a 7-DoF learned system in CasADi, ~40 ms / solve, on CPU.
  • Natural next steps. Hardware transfer, learning β and α from data, and composing the same filter with a flow-matching trajectory generator instead of a hand-designed min-snap reference.
